Bayesian Multilevel Analysis of Housing Prices in Major Ontario Cities
	How do interest rates, international student enrollment, and housing supply interact to drive housing prices across major Ontario cities?

Background and Motivation
	Housing affordability has become a major issue in Canada
	Interest rate changes from the Bank of Canada affect housing demand
	Growing international student inflows increase housing demand
	New housing supply varies across cities
	Housing markets respond differently across Ontario cities

Data Sources
	Dataset
		Panel dataset of 6 Major Ontario CMAs 
		From Time period:2016 to 2024
		Monthly observations
	Outcome Variable
		Housing price index (by city)
			log - percentage (%) changes
	Main Predictors
		Bank of Canada policy interest rate
		international study permits (proxy for student inflows)
		Housing starts (proxy for housing supply)
Bayesian Model and Expected Outputs
	Bayesian multilevel regression model
		Model "y_it​=α_i​+β_1​r_t​+β_2​student_t​+β_3​supply_it​+ϵ_it​"
		Partial pooling
	Bayesian Estimation
		Weakly informative priors
		Convergence diagnostics
		Posterior predictive checks
	Expected Outputs
		Posterior effects of interest rate, students, and housing supply
		City-level differences in housing market responses
		Posterior predictions of housing price trends
